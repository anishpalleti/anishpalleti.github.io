Free or Low-Cost Tax Help for Everyone

Get your taxes done accurately, maximize your refund, and avoid costly mistakes.

We help people understand eligibility for free programs, prepare required documents, and connect with local services that file free returns. Our goal is to reduce errors, prevent fraud, and make tax filing less stressful. Use our guides to check credits and deductions you may qualify for, learn what records to keep, and find step-by-step help for filing or responding to IRS notices.

Quick Info

Do You Qualify?

Eligibility

Low-to-moderate income, seniors, people with disabilities, and limited English speakers may qualify for free services. Eligibility criteria vary by program. Income limits, age, and the types of forms you need to file can affect which service is best for you, and we provide quick checks and links to find your nearest Volunteer Income Tax Assistance (VITA) site or Low Income Taxpayer Clinic (LITC) based on your situation.

Get Started

Gather Documents

W-2s, 1099s, Social Security numbers, receipts, and last year’s tax return if available. Bring documentation for income, healthcare coverage, and any tax credits you plan to claim (childcare, education, or energy credits). If self-employed, collect records of business income and expenses, 1099-NEC forms, and information about estimated tax payments.

Know Your Rights

Protect Yourself

Ensure preparers are registered and never sign a blank return. Keep copies of everything. Understand the Taxpayer Bill of Rights, which guarantees you fair treatment, privacy, and access to appeal processes if you disagree with IRS actions. If you suspect identity theft or fraud, follow our steps to secure your account and file any required identity-theft forms promptly.
