Protect Yourself From Fraud

Common Scams

  • Guaranteed refunds: No truthful preparer can promise a refund amount before reviewing your information.
  • Unsubstantiated refund claims: Preparers who guarantee large refunds often encourage inflating income or deductions. This can lead to penalties and criminal exposure.
  • Upfront fees and ghost services: Be cautious if a preparer demands cash up front and provides no credentials.
  • Identity theft and ghost preparers: Some criminals file fraudulent returns using stolen information. They may set up fake preparer operations to steal refunds.
  • Phishing emails and impersonation: The IRS will not initiate contact by email asking for payment.
  • Scam phone calls and threats: Scammers may threaten arrest or deportation. The IRS does not use threats to collect taxes.

Red Flags

  • Preparer won't give a copy of the return
  • Prepared returns with false information without explanation
  • Preparer asks you to sign a blank form

How to Report Fraud

IRS Tips Line: 1-800-366-4484. Use Form 14157 to report a questionable preparer. You can also contact local authorities and the IRS Office of Professional Responsibility.

To report identity theft or suspicious returns, use the IRS identity theft reporting tools and Form 14039 when instructed. Keep records of any communications and save copies of suspicious emails or messages.

Reporting helps authorities trace scams, recover lost funds when possible, and protect other taxpayers. If you believe your Social Security number was misused, contact the FTC and credit bureaus as well.
