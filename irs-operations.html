How the IRS Operates

  • The IRS enforces federal tax laws, processes returns, payments, and refunds.
  • It reviews returns for accuracy by comparing taxpayer reports with records from employers, banks, and financial institutions.
  • Discrepancies trigger automated or manual reviews to correct errors and resolve issues.
  • The IRS helps taxpayers understand tax laws through updated forms, guidance, online tools, and local assistance centers.

Main functions

  • Processing tax returns and issuing refunds in a secure, scheduled workflow.
  • Collection of unpaid taxes and managing taxpayer accounts, including installment agreements and offers in compromise.
  • Audits and examinations to verify accuracy and prevent fraud or evasion.
  • Providing taxpayer assistance and education through online resources, phone lines, and community partners like VITA.

Communication

The IRS primarily uses mail for official notices. It will not threaten immediate arrest or demand payment by unusual methods. Be cautious of phone, text, or email scams that impersonate the IRS.

Accept legitimate IRS correspondence by checking return addresses, using IRS online tools to verify notices, and contacting the IRS directly via published phone numbers if you are unsure.
