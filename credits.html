Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

Provides refundable credit to eligible low- and moderate-income workers. Eligibility depends on income, filing status, and number of qualifying children.

The credit amount and income limits change annually. Qualifying tests include adjusted gross income, investment income limits, and relationship/residence tests for children.

Common mistakes include reporting incorrect earned income, failing to apply the qualifying child rules correctly, or choosing the wrong filing status. These can cause an eligibility error or delay.

Child Tax Credit (CTC)

Credit for eligible parents for each qualifying child. Some or all of the credit may be refundable. Rules change periodically.

Eligibility depends on child age and relationship, earned income, and taxpayer's adjusted gross income. The refundable portion (ACTC) has earned-income thresholds and limits.

Mistakes often come from miscounting qualifying children, not reporting other income that affects phaseouts, or misunderstanding refundable vs. nonrefundable portions.
